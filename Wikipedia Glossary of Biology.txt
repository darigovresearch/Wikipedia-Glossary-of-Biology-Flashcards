"<dt class=""glossary"" id=""calvin_cycle"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-independent_reactions#Coupling_to_other_metabolic_pathways"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Light-independent reactions"">Calvin cycle</a></dfn></dt>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">The Calvin cycle, light-independent reactions, bio synthetic phase, dark reactions, or photosynthetic carbon reduction (PCR) cycle<sup id=""cite_ref-1"" class=""reference""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_biology#cite_note-1"">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photosynthesis"" title=""Photosynthesis"">photosynthesis</a> are the chemical reactions that convert carbon dioxide and other compounds into <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glucose"" title=""Glucose"">glucose</a>. These reactions occur in the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stroma_(fluid)"" title=""Stroma (fluid)"">stroma</a>, the fluid-filled area of a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloroplast"" title=""Chloroplast"">chloroplast</a> outside the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thylakoid_membranes"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Thylakoid membranes"">thylakoid membranes</a>. These reactions take the products (<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adenosine_triphosphate"" title=""Adenosine triphosphate"">ATP</a> and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NADPH"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""NADPH"">NADPH</a>) of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-dependent_reactions"" title=""Light-dependent reactions"">light-dependent reactions</a> and perform further chemical processes on them. There are three phases to the light-independent reactions, collectively called the <i>Calvin cycle</i>: carbon fixation, reduction reactions, and ribulose 1,5-bisphosphate (RuBP) regeneration.</dd>

"<dt class=""glossary"" id=""cell"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_(biology)"" title=""Cell (biology)"">cell</a></dfn></dt>"|"

A cell may exist as an independent, self-replicating unit (as in the case of <a href=""#unicellular""><span title=""See entry on this page at § unicellular"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">unicellular organisms</span></a>), or in cooperation with other cells, each of which may be specialized for carrying out particular functions within a larger <a href=""#multicellular""><span title=""See entry on this page at § multicellular"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">multicellular organism</span></a>. Cells consist of <a href=""#cytoplasm""><span title=""See entry on this page at § cytoplasm"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">cytoplasm</span></a> enclosed within a <a href=""#cell_membrane""><span title=""See entry on this page at § cell membrane"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">cell membrane</span></a> and sometimes a <a href=""#cell_wall""><span title=""See entry on this page at § cell wall"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">cell wall</span></a>, and serve the fundamental purpose of separating the controlled environment in which biochemical processes take place from the outside world. Most cells are visible only under a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microscope"" title=""Microscope"">microscope</a>.</dd>

<dd class=""glossary"">The branch of biology that studies the animal kingdom, including the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Structure"" title=""Structure"">structure</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embryology"" title=""Embryology"">embryology</a>, <a href=""#evolution""><span title=""See entry on this page at § evolution"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">evolution</span></a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_classification"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Scientific classification"">classification</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habits"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Habits"">habits</a>, and distribution of all animals, both living and extinct, and how they interact with their <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystems"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Ecosystems"">ecosystems</a>.</dd>
"
"<dt class=""glossary"" id=""zooplankton"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zooplankton"" title=""Zooplankton"">zooplankton</a></dfn></dt>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">Are <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterotroph"" title=""Heterotroph"">heterotrophic</a> (sometimes <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detritivore"" title=""Detritivore"">detritivorous</a>) <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plankton"" title=""Plankton"">plankton</a> (cf. <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytoplankton"" title=""Phytoplankton"">phytoplankton</a>). Plankton are organisms drifting in <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean"" title=""Ocean"">oceans</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea"" title=""Sea"">seas</a>, and bodies of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fresh_water"" title=""Fresh water"">fresh water</a>. Individual zooplankton are usually <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microscopic_scale"" title=""Microscopic scale"">microscopic</a>, but some (such as <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jellyfish"" title=""Jellyfish"">jellyfish</a>) are larger and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macroscopic_scale"" title=""Macroscopic scale"">visible</a> to the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naked_eye"" title=""Naked eye"">naked eye</a>.</dd>
"
"<dt class=""glossary"" id=""zygospore"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zygospore"" title=""Zygospore"">zygospore</a></dfn></dt>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diploid"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Diploid"">diploid</a> reproductive stage in the life cycle of many <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Fungi"">fungi</a> and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protists"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Protists"">protists</a>.</dd>
"
"<dt class=""glossary"" id=""zygote"" style=""margin-top: 0.4em;""><dfn class=""glossary""><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zygote"" title=""Zygote"">zygote</a></dfn></dt>"|"
<dd class=""glossary"">A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eukaryotic"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Eukaryotic"">eukaryotic</a> cell formed by a fertilization event between two <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gametes"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Gametes"">gametes</a>.</dd>
